The Metaverse is Just the Internet

It's been said before, I'll say it again: The "Metaverse" is another word for "Internet."

My guess is that you agree with me, but in case you don't I'll argue a bit. First, let's look at our diverse set of Metaverse definitions:

Snow Crash: The term 'metaverse' was coined by Neal Stephenson in the 1992 book Snow Crash. (Fun read. If you haven't read it yet, grab it from your online library.) The first web browser was released to the public only the year before.

Here's how Stephenson introduces the Metaverse:
So Hiro's not actually here at all. He's in a computer-generated universe that his computer is drawing onto his goggles and pumping into his earphones. In the lingo, this imaginary place is called the Metaverse. Hiro spends a lot of time in the Metaverse. It beats the shit out of the U-Stor-It.

Proper noun Metaverse. Not 'just the internet'. But close enough. Think Tik Tok, Instagram, social media. It's a retreat from the real world, better than facing life in a storage unit.

Snow Crash centers on a digitized mind-control drug, to be distributed to all users of the Metaverse (and granting control to one L. Bob Rife). Snow Crash is a warning, and it's a warning that applies equally to hours spent "inside" the two-dimensional Tik Tok as hours inside Meta's 3D worlds.

Andrew Ball: Andrew Ball is a VC who literally wrote the book on the metaverse. Shortly before all the covid-metaverse-hype, he published an in-depth blog defining the metaverse.

Before his definition, his caveat:

Just as it was hard to envision in 1982 what the Internet of 2020 would be - and harder still to communicate it to those who had never even "logged" onto it at that time - we don't really know how to describe the Metaverse.

I read that as: "The metaverse is whatever we end up with." (And if we still end up with the internet, well then the metaverse is the internet.)

Anyway, he provides 7 primary characteristics of the metaverse:

  1. Be persistent
  2. Be synchronous and live
  3. Be without any cap to concurrent users, while also providing each user with an individual sense of "presence"
  4. Be a fully functioning economy
  5. Be an experience that spans both the digital and physical worlds
  6. Offer unprecedented interoperability
  7. Be populated by "content" and "experiences" created and operated by an incredibly wide range of contributors

So now go back and read it again to convince yourself that this defines the metaverse, in addition to the internet. :)

None of the characteristics are even new features of the internet. We had MUD in 1980, the first online Visa payment was processed in 1994 - purchasing a physical CD. It's the most diverse platform ever invented.
